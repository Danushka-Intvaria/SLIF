Sector Overview

Sri Lanka offers a high-value proposition for education investors, driven by strong domestic and regional demand for higher and vocational education, a skilled workforce, and cost-efficient operations. The country has a literacy rate of 92%, one of the highest in South Asia, yet faces a significant gap in higher and vocational education, creating immediate investment opportunities. State higher education institutions currently enroll 44,000 students, producing over 30,000 graduates annually, while postgraduate programs graduate over 10,000 students per year . Sri Lanka’s strategic location in South Asia and the widespread use of English in education and business support international and transnational education models. Competitive costs for infrastructure, operations, and qualified academic staff further enhance investment viability. Under normal law, foreign investment in the education sector is restricted to a maximum 40% shareholding, but the Board of Investment (BOI) can approve higher foreign equity participation, subject to the approval of the Board, for Education sector projects. BOI-facilitated ventures also benefit from duty exemptions on capital equipment, and streamlined approvals, enabling investors to structure flexible and scalable education projects.

Why Invest in Sri Lanka

Higher Education &Vocational Education

Strong Unmet Demand

Sri Lanka boasts a 92% literacy rate and a large English-speaking youth population, withover 4 million school-aged students. Each year, more than 100,000 qualifiedstudentsare unable to access public universities, while another 100,000 await vocational training, creating strong demand for private higher education and skills development pathways. Growing sectors—including ICT, tourism, healthcare, manufacturing, andcreativeindustries—are driving a need for industry-aligned skills and applied education.

Cost-Competitive Regional Education Hub

Sri Lanka offers lower setup and operating costs compared to key regional educationhubs while maintaining quality standards. Affordable tuition and living costs attract bothdomestic and international students, supporting high enrollment potential andattractive returns for investors.

Skilled Academic & Training Workforce

The country has a strong base of qualified lecturers, trainers, and industry professionals. Strengths in STEM, ICT, healthcare, management, and applied sciences support practice- oriented and industry-linked education models.

English-Medium Advantage

English is widely used in higher education and professional training, enabling smoothintegration with international curricula. This creates strong opportunities fortransnational education, dual degrees, and branch campuses.

Regional Student Catchment

Sri Lanka is well positioned to attract students from South Asia, Maldives, theMiddleEast, and Africa. A safe, welcoming environment, good connectivity, and affordabilitystrengthen its appeal as a regional education destination.

Government & BOI Support

Education is a priority sector under national human capital development policies. The2026 education reforms emphasize vocational and technical education, creating asupportive investment climate. While general law limits foreign ownership to 40%, BOI- approved projects may allow more than 40% of foreign ownership, subject to approval.
